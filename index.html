Felix for Downtown Chair 20-21

"Alone we can do so little;
together we can do so much" ― Helen Keller

STRENGTHEN DOWNTOWN CROSS-PROGRAM COMMUNITY

Constant collaboration, net-working and strong-peer-bonds are a few of reasons BCIT Computing graduates are highly effective in the workplace.

Industry leaders organize as a super-tribe, and so can we!

Let's lay the cornerstone of a tradition of inter-program, inter-set, and inter-level collaboration.

BUILD BRIDGE BETWEEN BCITSA & ISEP

Every ISEP student has access to all of the BCITSA's services.

Spreading awareness about this, making services more accessible, and ensuring all ISEP students feel supported and part of the DTC community is important to me.

The Student Association works for every student, including you!

PROMOTE WELLNESS SERVICES

Making the most of your time at BCIT and getting the most out of your education requires students (and teachers!) to take wellness seriously.

Physical and mental wellbeing have a huge impact on our professional and personal lives.

I believe putting wellness services front and centre, for everyone at the DTC, is critical to impoving the experience of every student.

A PLEDGE TO LISTEN

My greatest strength is how little I know... Yes, really!

I believe I can serve the DTC student body much better by listening.

My current goals reflect what I have heard from my set, my cohort, my program, CST and Full-Stack students, international students, the current Downtown Chair Dawson Verboden, and the BCITSA councillors, executives and staff.

The greatest success I could hope for as Downtown Chair is for all students to feel heard and included by the BCITSA.
